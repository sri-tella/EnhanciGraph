What are Genes & Enhancers?

Genes are segments of DNA that contain instructions for building and maintaining the functional components of living organisms. They provide the code for synthesizing proteins, which are the building blocks of cells and perform various functions in the body. Genes are fundamental to the genetic makeup of an organism, influencing traits, behaviors, and physiological processes. Understanding genes is key to unraveling genetic diseases, inheritance patterns, and the overall functioning of living organisms.

Enhancers are regulatory DNA sequences that modulate the activity of nearby genes. They enhance or increase the transcription of genes by interacting with specific proteins and influencing the binding of RNA polymerase, an enzyme essential for gene transcription. Enhancers play a crucial role in spatial and temporal regulation of gene expression, ensuring that genes are activated in the right cells and at the right times. Studying enhancers provides insights into the complex network of gene regulation, tissue-specific expression patterns, and the dynamic control of genetic information.

Why are Gene-Enhancer links important?

Gene-Enhancer links refer to the physical interactions between enhancer regions and their target genes. These links establish the regulatory relationships that govern the precise timing and level of gene expression. Understanding gene-enhancer links is vital for deciphering the intricacies of gene regulation, cell differentiation, and the orchestration of biological processes. Identifying these links helps researchers comprehend how genetic information is utilized and controlled in various cellular contexts, providing insights into development, health, and disease.

BENGI Dataset: Enhancer-Gene Links

We have curated data from the Bengi dataset, focusing on enhancer-gene links in human genome across several tissues. The dataset is derived from the GTEx (Genotype-Tissue Expression) project, specifically the liver tissue, and utilizes the GTEX benchmark file. This benchmark file contains associations between enhancers and their corresponding genes, providing valuable insights into regulatory interactions within the genome.

  1. Data Curated from Git Repo: The Bengi dataset was sourced from a Git repository, ensuring the latest and most relevant data for analysis.
  2. Enhancer-Gene Mapping: The enhancer-gene links were established using the liver-specific data from the GTEX benchmark file. This information helps in understanding the regulatory relationships between enhancers and genes.
  3. CCRE Accession Numbers: Enhancer regions were identified using CCRE (Constitutive Cis-Regulatory Elements) accession numbers obtained from the human genome bed file (GRCh38.bedfile).
  4. Graphical Representation: Interactive graphs have been generated to visually represent the enhancer-gene links, providing an intuitive understanding of the regulatory landscape within the Bengi dataset.

ABC Dataset: Enhancer-Gene Links

Curated from the ABC Git repository, the dataset explores enhancer-gene links in the human genome across various tissues. The dataset comprises 131 unique cell types and 23,227 target genes, offering comprehensive insights into regulatory interactions.

  1. Data Curated from Git Repo: The ABC dataset is sourced from a Git repository, ensuring up-to-date and relevant data for analysis.
  2. Graph Generation: Graphs are generated by considering chromosome start and end as enhancer regions, with associated target genes forming nodes.
  3. ABC Score-Based Edges: Graph edges between enhancers and genes are weighted associated with ABC scores. Low ABC scored edges are represented in red, medium in yellow and high in green.
  4. Color Mappings: Nodes are color-coded, with light blue representing enhancer regions and green representing target genes.
